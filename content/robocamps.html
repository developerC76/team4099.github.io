About

During RoboCamps, elementary and middle school students, ages 8-14, will learn and devleop math, engineering, and computer science skills in the context of competitive robotics in an interactive and friendly environment. This year, RoboCamps will be hosted online over Zoom, but students will still have the chance to physically build at home.

RoboCamps will consist of five online sessions, each five hours long, with a lunch break in between. Campers will work with the help of their counselors, including mentors, subteam leads, and veterans, to construct a robotics kit that they receive from Poolesville High School. Throughout the course, campers will obtain the invaluable skills of designing, building, and coding a simple robot, as well as receive the feeling of accomplishment from watching it successfully run for the first time.

We offer both beginner and intermediate RoboCamps sessions, so there is a program for everyone. Read further to learn more about the material being taught in these respective camps.

Beginner

This session allows campers to build and program a basic VEX IQ robot from the comfort of their own home. Campers will learn about block code, sensors, and more to enable their robot to complete a challenge. At the end of the week, campers will participate in a virtual competition!

Intermediate

This session delves deeper into text-based code, sensors, and mechanism design for campers to design, build, and program a VEX IQ robot at home. They will apply these skills to complete several challenges. At the end of the week, campers will participate in a virtual competition!

Logistics and General Information

Camps will be held every day in the session over a Zoom call. Sessions will be held from 9:00 AM to 3:00 PM with a 1 hour lunch break in between. Counselors will also have office hours where campers can ask questions. More information about joining sessions will be sent 1 week before your selected session. The VEX robotics kits that will be used during these sessions must be picked up one week prior to the start of your course at the church near Poolesville High School. As the date gets closer, emails will be sent out regarding specific times. The registration fee for one week is $175 plus a $20 deposit that we will return to you once you have returned the kit to the team. If you refer a friend who signs up for our camps (indicated during the registration process), your price goes down to $165! In addition to the registration fee and sign up, the Online Communication and Media Consent form must be completed and can be found here as well as the liability form which can be found here. If you would like financial support, please fill out the form found here. Please email all forms to robocamps@team4099.com after registering. Our refund policy is as follows: if you withdraw your registration two weeks prior to the course, you will get a full refund. Withdrawing one week prior will give a $60 refund. Anything sooner to the start date of your course will not allow for a refund.

FAQ

Q: Who are the camp instructors?

A: The camp instructors will consist of adult mentors as well as student members of Team 4099. All instructors have mastery over the content they are teaching.

Q. How is this different than in-person RoboCamps?

A: Due to the COVID-19 pandemic, we unfortunately cannot run RoboCamps in person this year due to social distancing and health risks. Although, we still have the program available, it will just be online. Students will be able to pick up kits from Poolesville High and build their own robot at home. They will be mentored online over Zoom. The kits will still have to be returned.

Q: What will a RoboCamps session look like?

A: Sessions will have 10 participants. The session opens with an interactive lecture led by a mentor or counselor, where everybody is able to ask questions. We then break up into groups of 4 students and 1 counselor, where students ask questions, do interactive work designed to hone the skills taught in the lecture, and discuss in a small group.

Q: Who are RoboCamps meant for?

A: RoboCamps are meant for older elementary school aged students and above who want to get started with robotics, design or programming.

Q: My child has no experience with robotics, or programming. Can they still participate?

A: Yes! Our camp is geared towards students of all ages and all experience levels. There are no specific prerequisites, but make sure your camper is comfortable with most of the beginner topics before they sign up for a intermediate session.

Q: I am interested in attending RoboCamps, but cost is an issue.

A: We understand that during the current economic situation, the $175 cost may be difficult for some to pay. If cost is a barrier to you for registration, please email us and we can discuss options. Team 4099’s goal is to help children and teams learn, regardless of economic circumstances.

Q: How do refunds work?

A: Refunds differ based on how long in advance you are canceling:
  • If you are withdrawing 2 weeks in advance, you will get a full refund.
  • If you are withdrawing 1 week in advance, you will get $60 back.
  • If you are withdrawing less than 1 week in advance, we do not offer refunds.

Q: Will you ship the kits to the students?

A: Unfortunately during this time we are not able to. You can pick up your kit the week prior to the first course at the church near Poolesville High School.
