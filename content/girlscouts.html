RoboBadge, The Scouts Badge Program

An Overview Of RoboBadge

Scouts will get the opportunity to earn their robotics badges (Programming Robots, Designing Robots, Showcasing Robots)! This event is open to all Brownies, Juniors, and Cadettes of Girl Scouts. Team 4099 will have their students on site as volunteers who will teach and assist the students.

Who We Are

Team 4099 is a World Championship attending FIRST Robotics Competition (FRC) team based in Poolesville, Maryland. We apply our knowledge of Science, Technology, Engineering, and Math (STEM) to build a robot for a challenge given by the FIRST Robotics Competition. Students not only partake in the competition aspect of the team, but also focus on spreading STEM and robotics awareness in their communities. Team 4099 strives to create an inclusive learning community where students can learn and apply their knowledge in STEM to real-world problems.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Where can I register?

A: You can register at the links above. Choose the link for the session you want to register for. Once you pay you will be redirected to a Google Form to enter information about your child. Be sure to fill out the emergency procedures form, the emergency medical information form and the media consent form (if applicable)!

Q: Are the badges provided?

A: Yes! Each girl takes home their three respective badges at the end of the session!

Q: Which Scouts are eligible to participate in RoboBadge?

A: Our camps will be open to all Brownies, Juniors, and Cadettes of Girl Scouts.

Q: Where will RoboBadge be held?

A: Check back in Fall 2019 for specific locations, it will most likely be near your troop

Q: What is the cost of one session of RoboBadge?

A: The cost to attend one week of RoboBadge is $15. This helps cover the cost of the badges we provide.

Q: Is there a waitlist?

A: Yes. You will automatically be placed on the waitlist if the session you have signed up for is full. We will email you if any spots open up.

Q: Is there a deadline to register?

A: Yes. You must register a week before the start date of your session. If there are not enough students registered by then, we will cancel that session (a full refund will be provided for anyone registered prior to then).

Q: Is lunch provided for the students?

A: Lunch will not be provided.

Q: How many students are in each session?

A: Each session will have at maximum 50 students. The actual amount of students will vary per session.

Q: Who are the camp instructors?

A: The camp instructors will consist of adult mentors as well as student members of Team 4099. All instructors have experience in the FIRST Robotics Competition as well as what to teach the students.

Q: What is the cancellation policy?

A: Refunds will not be offered

Q: Do the students take the robots home?

A: No, the kits that the students use to build the robots will be needed for the other sessions.

If you have any questions, feel free to email us at robocamps@team4099.com!
